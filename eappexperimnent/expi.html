What is a Green Information Technology (GIT)?

Green Information Technology (GIT) is an environmental resource management policy that gives policymakers a new topic of discussion to encourage green technologies that reduce global high mass carbon emissions (Nizam, 2020). Moreover, according to Taklo (2020), the substantial threat that environmental degradation poses to human survival has led communities and organizations to prioritize green innovation as a means of protecting the environment and fostering economic development. Hence, GIT practices aim to minimize hazardous materials such as electronic waste (e-waste), enhance energy efficiency, and promote the biodegradability of unused and outdated products.


Objectives

1. To promote the usage of green IT.

2.To discover that this kind of innovation is feasible.

3.To lessen e-waste.


Goal: To minimize the impact of e-waste by promoting recycling and responsible disposal of waste and encouraging people to use renewable resources such as solar panels.
